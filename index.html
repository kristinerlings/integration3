About the Sheng instrument

A blast from the past

The Sheng's Centuries-Old History

The Sheng, a traditional Chinese instrument, is said to have been created by the goddess Nü Wa, who is known as the creator of all humans.

Not only can it produce sound while breathing in and out, but it can also play multiple notes at once, making it a unique and versatile instrument.

The Sheng is made of bamboo sticks and has five main parts: the "sheng chest", "mouthpiece", "sheng pipes" which are placed in the chest with "finger-holes" and reeds placed on each pipe to produce individual notes.

From Terpodians to the Aura

The first harmonica

Christian Frederich Buschmann, a German instrument maker, was exploring ways to simplify the tuning of pianos,when he created the first harmonica.

The harmonica was called the Aura, and was inspired by a small device used for tuning, called Terpodians.

Built of 21 metal reeds, the Aura had yet to evolve, as currently it could only play notes when blown.

About how the modern harmonica came to be

Making Music History

The Modern Harmonica

Joseph Richter introduced the first diatonic harmonica featuring both blow and draw notes by adding a second set of reeds. This marked a significant turning point in the evolution of the instrument

The modern diatonic harmonica, as we know it today, is characterised by its 10 holes and 2 reed plates, each equipped with 10 metal reeds. Depending on where you are located, the diatonic harmonica can be referred as the blues harp in the West or as the tremolo harmonica in East..
